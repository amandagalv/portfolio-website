The Problem

While Generation Z and Millennials are more likely to invest in the stock market today than previous generations, there is a gap in the market for products that ‘speak their language’ and reflect their habits, such as connecting and reaching to others online for support.

The Process>

I worked part time from Dec 2020 – Jun 2021 on a multi-step process (visualised below) that allowed me to explore different stages of a full-cycle project.

1. Emphasise

Competitive Analysis

To understand the market around the stock market, I conducted a competitive analysis of Revolut and Reddit. I used the S.W.O.T (Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities and Threats) method which helped me identify gaps in the market and possible concepts ideas for filling them. Further research showed that there is a growing interest in investing among younger generations.

User Survey

I ran a survey study with participants between 25-40 years old. The survey was a great method to collect data relatively quick.

Key Survey Findings

Where do you put your savings? (you can select more than one answer)

Have you ever invested money through any method?

How comfortable are you talking about money?

Does others’ successful investments influence you?

User Interviews

Participants

    6 participants

    28-37 years old

     Familiar with UK online banking platforms

Key Insights

Financial Interest

Most non-experts like the idea of using financial tools to grow their savings but they don’t have a personal interest in finance.
SOLUTION: A personalised feature can help users to keep updated on the news that matter to their own investments and goals.

Social Habits

Users appreciate and feel more confidant when seeing other investors’ moves, but most users might prefer a private account using nicknames and no photos so that they feel comfortable sharing their experiences.
SOLUTION: A social-media feature where investors can exchange ideas while keeping their real identity private can be helpful to users.

Impediment

Users who are not financial experts feel overwhelmed with the excessive amount of technical information. That is often the reason why they give up on investing.
SOLUTION: Less technical terms and allowing users to see the acvitity of more experient investors can help new users to feel confident and give them a start point.

2. Define

User Personas

Based on the findings from my user research, I created two personas, Sara and Matt, to help put focus on the needs and frustations to be addressed. Sara has a spontaneous personality, more risk-friendly and focused on the moment. Matt likes to have a plan and a long-term goal to aim for.

User JOurneys

To understand better the experience Sara and Matt would have with Pluto and what features could be helpful to them, I created a customer journey map for both.

The Testing Process

6 participants, all were:

    28-40 years old Familiar

    Familiar to UK online banking

     Remotely interviewed using Zoom

Goal:

    To observe and test if the app is easy to navigate

     To observe if the features and buttons felt intuitive and easy to access

After writing and developing a test script, I organised the data in a rainbow spreadsheet, using the Jakob Nielsen method of severity ratings to grade any usability problems.​​​​​​​ I also created an affinity map with the most common comments and observations.

Refining the Prototype

Using a combination of pen, paper and Figma I gradually elevated the fidelity of my prototypes. Through user testing, preference testing and peer reviews, I had enough data to refine the design.

01 | Home screen

Through comments and ‘thinking out loud observations on the first round of usability test, I noticed that some users found some screens to blend, lacking colour, so I added colours and icons creating a more visually interesting interface but also recognition through the icons. I ran a preference test as I was unsure how to name the screen. 8 of 13 participants preferred the screen to have ‘Pluto’ header instead of ‘Home’.

02 | Stocks

The wireframe had a top header indicating the name of the page, and a ‘go back’ button.
On the High-Fidelity Prototypes prototype, the whole platform structure was amended to a more horizontal sitemap. That helped user to locate themselves and reduce the need for too many ‘go back’ clicks. I achieved this by creating a consistent 3 layer structure for Pluto: Main sections, sub’sections and filter.
The bottom tab link the user to the 3 main sections (Home, Feed, Wealth) of the app. The top header shows the name of this section. Below it, buttons take the user to any sub-sections (stocks, savings, groups, etc.) and highlights the current location. On a third row, a side-slide list of filter quicky take the user to specific screens (Trending, My Stocks, Specific groups, etc).

03 | Groups

User used to go inside Feed, Groups, My Groups, Chosen Group to read their group threads. On this path, there was no interesting hint or tip such as a trending topic. On the usability testing it was noted by several users that the path didn’t add them any ‘info/surprise’ on the way, feeling unecessarily long.
Just as we’ve seen on stocks, on the High-Fidelity Prototype the whole platform structure was amended to a more horizontal sitemap. That helped user to locate themselves and reduced the need for ‘empty’ pages on the user’s path.

Conclusion

Key Findings

Research pays off.
The feedback and insights from both user reserch and usability testing were an extremely valuable source to show how Pluto could stand out and be more helpful to users.

Use rules for consistency since the beginning.
I used a grid system from the beggining and that was a big time-saver when refining Pluto. For my next projects, I will also set part of the design documentation, such as typography and colour palette, from the beggining so the design evolutes more consistent and there’s less need to refine afterwards.

Consider Accessibility.
It’s important to choosing the typography and interface colours while keeping accessibility in mind.

Next Steps

With more rounds of usability testing, the Feed feature could be improved and possibly more gamified. More features could be shown to be useful and/or appreciated by users.

At some point, a feature for Pension could be added so users have all their financial wealth in one place (savings, pension, investments).
